Monster Attributes and What They Mean

Type

The category that best describes the monster's behavior.

  • Node: Monsters that follow a certain nodular path, and don't deviate from it.
  • Main: Monsters that when an expendable is spotted, will either actively pursue them until they hide or open the next door, or don't fit into any of the other categories.
  • Joke: Monsters that may or may not be harmful, rarely encountered, only added as a joke.
  • Conditional: Monsters that only attack the expendable if a certain condition is met.
  • Grand: Monsters that are considered exponentially more dangerous than Main monsters, they act as the "bosses" of Pressure.
  • Not: Monsters that their first action is not to harm the expendable, but have the ability to do so.

Alignment

The general behavior or stance of the monster towards the expendable.

  • Hostile: Monsters that will actively seek out and harm the expendable on sight.
  • Neutral: Monsters that may not actively seek out the expendable, but will harm them if provoked or if they come too close.
  • Docile: Monsters that do not wish to harm the expendable.
  • Conditional: A branch of neutral that will only harm the expendable if a certain condition is met.

Rarity

How often a monster is encountered within a run.

  • Guaranteed: The monster is guaranteed to appear every so often.
  • Common: Monsters that appear frequently throughout a run, but vary in exact frequency.
  • Uncommon: Monsters that appear less frequently than common monsters, but more often than rare ones.
  • Rare: Monsters that may only appear once or twice in a run.
  • Elusive: Monsters that appear very rarely, even once within a run.
  • Conditional: Monsters that will only appear if a certain condition is met.

Damage

The amount of damage a monster can inflict on the expendable per attack (100 is max HP, 0 is lowest).

Speed

How fast a monster can move towards the expendable.

  • Slow: Monsters that move really slowly.
  • Normal: Monsters that move at a decent speed, can get the expendable if no action is taken.
  • Fast: Monsters that move quickly, require quick reactions from the expendable.
  • Immediate: Monsters that reach the expendable almost instantly, require perfect reactions.
  • Irrelevant: Monsters that do not move towards the expendable.
  • Conditional: Monsters whose speed varies based on certain conditions.

Mode

What gamemodes a monster appears in.

  • Story: Monsters that appear in story mode only.
  • Story/Endless: Monsters that appear in both story mode and endless mode.
  • Endless: Monsters that appear in endless mode only.
  • Hunted: Monsters that appear in The Hunted mode only.
  • Raveyard: Monsters that appear in the Raveyard mode only.
  • All: Monsters that appear in every mode.

Rebounds

If a monster will rebound if it reaches the end of a room.

  • Yes: The monster will rebound.
  • No: The monster will not rebound.

Sex

The biological sex of the monster, if applicable.

  • Male: The monster has been confirmed to be male.
  • Female: The monster has been confirmed to be female.
  • Unknown: The monster has a biological sex, but which one has not been confirmed.
  • Sexless: The monster does not have a biological sex.
  • Both: The monster can be either male or female, depending on the individual.
